    Bhootharadhane

     

    Bhootharadhane is widely popular because of its unique spectacle and strange aura. Bhootha Aradhane Widely practiced in the coastal regions of Karnataka,The folk origins of the ritual is clear and is carried on undisturbed as they were in the ancient times.The festival is a great mix of folk beliefs, awesome spectacle and ritualistic magic to ward away the evil.

    Bhootha Aradhane of Karnataka involves much visual splendor. Typically, it involves a procession in which idols are carried with great pomp. The procession of Bhootharadhane reaches a climax when the procession reaches the podium amidst great cheer made by the beating drums and the firecrackers.

    "Patri"who is priest of the ceremony begins a dance and gradually gets into a state of trance and starts prophesying, apparently with the help of the "Bhoota" that temporarily possesses him. In his possessed state, he often settles quarrels within the community and also extends promises of prosperity.

    This documentary is made takes a tour of this great experience.This was produced for CIIL's Kannada Bhasha Mandakini project ind the year 2006.

    Central Institute of Indian Languages was established to co-ordinate the development of Indian Languages, to bring about the essential unity of Indian languages through scientific studies, Promote inter-disciplinary research, contribute to mutual enrichment of languages, and thus contribute towards emotional integration of people of India.
